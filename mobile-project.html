Foodie Buddy Mobile App Project

Project Overview

The project is the final mobile application project from IAT359, Mobile Computing. The application is Foodie Buddy, a food focus social app that lets users leave reviews of the restaurants and post them on the feed. The app incorporates the camera of the phone to let users take photos when creating a post; alternatively, the users can use the photos in the phone gallery to do so. The app also lets users search nearby restaurants with the built-in map.

The issue of this solution is it doesn't bring new interactivity to the museum, as the museum still has to rely on the same specimen and activity it already has, just a different way to display them. This solution merely makes the museum more approachable, and not much else.

Second Solution: Arcade Machines

We re-examine the case and find out that the museum itself doesn't have too many interactive installations, and we wish to focus on this issue next. The main way that the visitors interact with the museum is to read the plaque on the display case, and occasionally there are some monitors nearby to provide additional information about the species around them; but all in all, the visitors have to do lots of reading to gain knowledge, which is more in line with the traditional museum. Through our observation, we realized some of the younger visitors tend to get bored with what the museum is offering, so we came up with a solution for them to learn through gameplay. The arcade machines are the proposal we created. Each machine came with different video games to learn about ecological topics such as endangered species and their shrinking habitat. The proposal is on the right track; however, since we frame the children as the target audience this time, we ignore a large portion of the visitors. We aim to bring a broader audience for our next solution.

Final Solution: Interactive panel

During our visit to the museum, we found that the museum is fairly static, the visitors typically leave after they view some of the most impressive species before they even walk through every aisle (most of the aisle contains dead aquatic species preserved in jars, which admittedly they are not the most exciting things to see). We want to retain the visitor a bit longer by bringing in motion into the museum space

The end result was largely a success, the map was functioning after the downgrading, although I have to spend some time to change all the affected plugins and functionalities of the app as well.
